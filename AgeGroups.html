    Age Groups

    This page looks at the different age groups and their patterns in both substance use and mental health.

    Drug Use

    • Ages 12-17: Captures the initial patterns of drug and alcohol use in teens. These ages are critical for prevention programs because they are early.
    • Ages 18-25: A period when substance experimentation occurs. There are higher prevalence rates compared to the other age groups.
    • Age 26+: Largest demographic group surveyed, patterns reflect more long-term use behaviors and show how substance use changes over the lifespan.

    Mental Illness

    • NOTE: There is no information available in the dataset for ages 12-18. This is why we included only 18-25 and 26+ estimates.
    • As shown in the table above, mental illness also shows high percentages between ages 18-25, compared to ages 26+.

    Drug Use Vs. Mental Health

    The data allow us to explore relationships between substance use and mental health through age groups. Research consistently shows that individuals reporting higher levels of illicit drug use also report higher rates of mental health issues. By visualizing drug use alongside mental health indicators, we can better understand how health challenges compound each other and identify age groups with the most significant risk.
