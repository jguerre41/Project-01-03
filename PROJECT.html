    Substance Use and Mental Health In America

    The visualizations on this website are based on data from the National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH), one of the most widely used public health surveys in the United States. The NSDUH is conducted annually by the Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA), a federal agency within the US Department of Health and Human Services (HHS).

    Survey Year and Release Information

    The data was released under the 2023 National Survey on Drug Use and Health, which includes 2022 and 2023 estimates where applicable. The tables provide estimates for all 50 US states and the District of Columbia. SAMHSA provides statistical modelling techniques, such as confidence intervals and regression models with demographic comparisons, such as age ranges.

    The purpose of this survey is to measure the prevalence of substance use and mental health conditions among the US population aged 12 and older. This includes people living in households, college dorms, and boarding houses; it just excludes active-duty military personnel, incarcerated individuals, and individuals experiencing homelessness who are not currently in shelters.
