    Different Drugs

    This page looks at alcohol, marijuana, and cocaine, to explore the relationships between these drugs through visualizations.

    Alcohol

    • Legal substance widely consumed in the US
    • NSDUH measures binge drinking and heavy drinking.
    • Alcohol is included because of its widespread use and its significant impact on public and mental health.
    • Similar pattern in the following drugs is the Northeast region has the highest alcohol use percentages, with Vermont leading at 59.07%.

    Marijuana

    • Commonly illicit drug in the US, with variations in legal status depending on the state.
    • In the case of Marijuana use, some of the highest percentages are distributed between the Northeast and the West.
    • The highest percentage states include Oregon (30.51%), Nevada (29.76%), New Mexico (29.96%), and Colorado (29.61%).
    • Lowest percentages include Texas(16.41%) and Iowa(15.45%).

    Cocaine

    • A stimulant drug tracked by NSDUH, including all forms(powder, crack)
    • Although less prevalent than marijuana and alcohol, cocaine remains a key indicator of substance use trends.
    • We have percentages ranging between 1.5 and 2.8. We have some of the highest states in the North, including Colorado (2.48), Illinois (2.34), and Rhode Island (2.28). The highest state is in the West region, being Oregon, with a 2.8%
    • The lowest percentages are in states like Arkansas, Georgia, and Utah, with the lowest at 1.21.
